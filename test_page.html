EGFR Mutant NSCLC Response to Fourth-Generation Inhibitors: A Multicenter Retrospective Analysis

Zhang W, Patel AR, Nakamura Y, Okafor CE
Published March 15, 2024 · DOI: 10.1200/JCO.2024.00000
Download: Full Text PDF Supplementary Data (PDF) Tables S1–S4 (DOCX)

Abstract

Fourth-generation EGFR inhibitors targeting C797S and compound mutations represent the next frontier in treating NSCLC following osimertinib resistance. We retrospectively analyzed 214 patients across six centers who received investigational fourth-generation agents after documented osimertinib failure, evaluating objective response rate, progression-free survival, and circulating tumor DNA dynamics.

Introduction

Non-small cell lung cancer driven by EGFR mutations accounts for approximately 15% of NSCLC in Western populations and up to 50% in East Asian cohorts. Third-generation inhibitors, particularly osimertinib, have dramatically improved outcomes, yet acquired resistance invariably emerges, most commonly via the C797S tertiary mutation.

Methods

All patients had centrally confirmed EGFR-mutant NSCLC with documented radiologic progression on osimertinib. Molecular profiling was performed via ctDNA NGS (Guardant360) and tissue biopsy where feasible. See Supplementary Methods for full protocol details.

Results

The overall ORR was 38% (95% CI: 31–45%). Median PFS was 5.4 months (IQR 3.1–8.7). Full response data stratified by mutation subtype are provided in Table S1. Kaplan-Meier curves are shown in Supplementary Figure S1.
