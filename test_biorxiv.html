HBM4 hybrid bonding enables 3D-stacked compute: implications for AI accelerator supply chains and the semiconductor equipment ecosystem

Priya Sundaram, Dong-Hyun Lee, Marco Pellegrini
bioRxiv 2024.06.01.596789 · Posted June 1, 2024 · doi: https://doi.org/10.1101/2024.06.01.596789

Download

PDF Full Text

Abstract

The transition to HBM4 memory introduces hybrid bonding as a critical enabling technology, reducing bump pitch below 10 µm and enabling die-to-wafer stacking geometries incompatible with conventional mass reflow processes. We model the capital equipment implications for advanced packaging, identifying BE Semiconductor Industries (BESI) and ASMPT as primary beneficiaries of volume ramp through 2026–2027, driven by thermocompression bonder adoption curves at leading memory and logic foundries.
