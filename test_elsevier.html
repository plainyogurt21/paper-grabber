Osimertinib resistance mechanisms in EGFR-mutant NSCLC: a systematic review and meta-analysis

Jiyeon Kim, Antonio Russo, Fabrice Barlesi
Cancer Cell, Volume 41, Issue 11, November 2023, Pages 1892–1908
Download full text PDF View PDF

Abstract

Osimertinib, a third-generation EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitor, has become the standard first-line therapy for EGFR-mutant NSCLC. However, resistance invariably develops through diverse mechanisms including on-target mutations (C797S, L718Q), bypass signaling activation (MET amplification, HER2 amplification), and histologic transformation. We conducted a systematic review of 84 studies encompassing 3,241 patients to characterize the landscape of acquired resistance mechanisms and emerging therapeutic strategies.

Keywords

EGFR; osimertinib; resistance; NSCLC; C797S; MET amplification; fourth-generation inhibitors
